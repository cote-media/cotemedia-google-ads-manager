# LoraMer — Glossary & Tooltips

Source content for three surfaces, built in one focused pass later:
1. Inline "ⓘ" tooltips next to fields/terms in the app.
2. First-run onboarding (each relevant tip shown once on first use).
3. A persistent Glossary / Help page (always accessible).

Each entry = the field/term and a plain-language note written to HAMMER HOME how LoraMer turns the detail
into structured data, a better view of the business, and better growth recommendations — purpose first,
then examples. Collected as spotted; tweak wording freely before the build.

## Client Profile — every field (surfaces: inline ⓘ + first-run onboarding + glossary)

**What this business does** — The single most important thing you can tell Lora: it's how she frames every
number. "Ecomm" tells her little; "modular foam furniture for kids, sold DTC" tells her your margins are
physical-product, your buyers are parents, your season skews to back-to-school and holidays. The sharper
this is, the sharper her reads and recommendations. Examples: "B2B SaaS for dental offices, ~$200/mo,
60-day sales cycle." "DTC running shoes for people with foot pain."

**Service area** — Where your customers are, so Lora reads geography, seasonality, and benchmarks correctly.
A regional spike, a cold-weather dip, or "why is California converting better" only make sense once she
knows your footprint. Examples: "Nationwide (US)." "Local — Atlanta metro." "Global, English-speaking."

**Website** — Two jobs: it grounds Lora in what you actually sell, and it's what she scans to learn your
offers, positioning, and tone so she starts informed instead of blind. Just the URL — Lora reads it and
tells you what she sees.

**NAICS code(s)** — Optional but powerful: it pins your business to a standardized industry definition Lora
knows cold, sharpening her benchmarks and seasonality expectations. Add more than one if you span
categories. Examples: search "pool" -> 561790; "masonry" -> 238140.

**Connections** — Every source you connect is another lens on the same business, and LoraMer's whole edge is
reading them together. One platform is a keyhole; Google + Meta + Shopify + Analytics together is the room.
The more you connect, the more complete the picture and the better the cross-channel recommendations.

**Rules** — Hard guardrails Lora obeys on every answer, no exceptions — for anything that would otherwise
lead her astray. They override her defaults. Examples: "Ignore ROAS — no purchase tracking." "Report in
CAD." "Primary metric is CPL, target $35."

**Facts** — Durable truths that make every answer personal instead of generic. Lora keeps them forever and
learns more on her own (from your data, chats, and the website scan) for you to confirm. The richer this
gets, the more she sounds like someone who actually knows the account. Examples: "Busy season is Nov-Dec."
"Margins ~60% on the core line." "Launching a new product in Q2."
